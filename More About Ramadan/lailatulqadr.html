Laylat-ul-Qadr (لیلة القدر) – The Night of Power

What is Laylat-ul-Qadr?
Laylat-ul-Qadr, also known as the Night of Power, Decree, or Destiny, is the most sacred and blessed night in Islam. It is the night when the first verses of the Holy Qur’an were revealed to Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) through Angel Jibreel (Gabriel). It falls within the last ten nights of Ramadan, specifically on one of the odd nights (21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, or 29th night).

Why is Laylat-ul-Qadr Important?
📖 Allah says in the Qur’an:
"Indeed, We sent the Qur’an down during the Night of Decree. And what can make you know what the Night of Decree is? The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months. The angels and the Spirit [Jibreel] descend therein by permission of their Lord for every matter. Peace it is until the emergence of dawn."
(Surah Al-Qadr 97:1-5)

Key Blessings of Laylat-ul-Qadr:
🔹 Better than 1,000 months (equivalent to 83+ years of worship).
🔹 The night when the Qur’an was revealed.
🔹 Angels descend to bring peace and blessings.
🔹 Dua (supplication) is accepted.
🔹 Sins are forgiven for those who worship sincerely.

When is Laylat-ul-Qadr?
The exact date of Laylat-ul-Qadr is unknown, but Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) instructed us to seek it in the last ten nights of Ramadan, particularly on the odd nights:
📅 21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, or 29th night of Ramadan.
📖 The Prophet (ﷺ) said:
"Seek Laylat-ul-Qadr in the last ten nights of Ramadan, in the odd nights."
(Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim)

🔹 Many scholars believe it is most likely on the 27th night of Ramadan, but it is best to worship during all the last ten nights to ensure receiving its blessings.

Signs of Laylat-ul-Qadr 🔹 The night is calm, peaceful, and pleasant.
🔹 The moon appears soft and radiant.
🔹 The sky is clear without strong winds or storms.
🔹 No shooting stars are seen in the sky.
🔹 The morning after Laylat-ul-Qadr, the sun rises softly without strong rays.

📖 The Prophet (ﷺ) said:
"The sun rises the next morning without rays, appearing like a disk."
(Sahih Muslim)

How to Make the Most of Laylat-ul-Qadr? Since this night is full of blessings and forgiveness, Muslims should engage in extra worship:

1️⃣ Perform Tahajjud (Night Prayers) & Nawafil
Praying Taraweeh and extra voluntary prayers will bring immense rewards.

📖 The Prophet (ﷺ) said:
"Whoever prays on Laylat-ul-Qadr out of sincere faith and seeking reward, all his past sins will be forgiven."
(Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim)

2️⃣ Recite & Reflect on the Qur’an
Since the Qur’an was revealed on Laylat-ul-Qadr, it is the best time to recite, understand, and reflect on its meaning.

3️⃣ Make Duas (Supplications) Sincerely
This is the best night for making heartfelt supplications (duas). The most recommended dua is:

📖 Aisha (RA) asked the Prophet (ﷺ):
"O Messenger of Allah, if I find Laylat-ul-Qadr, what should I say?"
He replied:
🛑 اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ العَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي
"Allahumma innaka ‘afuwwun tuhibbul-‘afwa fa’fu ‘anni"
💬 "O Allah, You are Most Forgiving, and You love forgiveness, so forgive me."
(Sunan Ibn Majah & Tirmidhi)

4️⃣ Give Charity (Sadaqah & Zakat)
Donating on Laylat-ul-Qadr is like giving charity for 83+ years!

5️⃣ Do Dhikr (Remembrance of Allah)
SubhanAllah (سبحان الله) – Glory be to Allah
Alhamdulillah (الحمد لله) – All praise is for Allah
Allahu Akbar (الله أكبر) – Allah is the Greatest
La ilaha illa Allah (لا إله إلا الله) – There is no god but Allah
6️⃣ Repent & Seek Forgiveness
Ask Allah to forgive your past sins and grant you a righteous future.

Conclusion;
Laylat-ul-Qadr is the most powerful night of the year. It offers immeasurable rewards, blessings, and forgiveness. Since we do not know the exact date, it is important to seek it in the last ten nights of Ramadan, especially on the odd nights.

✅ Worship sincerely
✅ Make dua for yourself and your loved ones
✅ Repent and seek forgiveness
✅ Give charity and help the poor

May Allah grant us the ability to witness Laylat-ul-Qadr, accept our prayers, and forgive our sins. Ameen! 🤲✨
