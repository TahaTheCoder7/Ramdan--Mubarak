What is Taraweeh?

Taraweeh (تراویح) is a special night prayer performed by Muslims during Ramadan.
It is offered after the obligatory Isha prayer and is considered a Sunnah Mu’akkadah (highly recommended but not obligatory) in Islam. This prayer is unique to Ramadan
and provides an opportunity for Muslims to earn extra rewards and blessings.
Significance of Taraweeh
Spiritual Upliftment: It is a time for self-reflection, repentance, and drawing closer to Allah.
Recitation of the Qur’an: The Qur’an is recited extensively in Taraweeh, and many mosques complete the
entire Qur’an (Khatm-ul-Quran) throughout
Ramadan. Following the Sunnah: The Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) prayed Taraweeh and encouraged his followers to do the same.
Forgiveness of Sins: According to Hadith, whoever prays during Ramadan with sincerity and faith will have their past sins forgiven.
How Many Rak’ahs in Taraweeh? The number of Rak’ahs (units of prayer) varies. The most common are:
8 Rak’ahs: Some follow the practice of Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), who prayed 8 Rak’ahs.
20 Rak’ahs: Many mosques around the world, especially in Hanafi tradition, pray 20 Rak’ahs.
More or Less: Some individuals or mosques may pray more, depending on their stamina and devotion.
How is Taraweeh Performed? Performed after Isha Prayer.
Prayed in sets of two Rak’ahs.
Short or Long Recitations: Some Imams recite shorter portions of the Qur’an, while others recite longer sections to complete the full Qur’an during
Ramadan. Can Be Prayed in Congregation or Alone: While praying in a mosque with a congregation is encouraged, one can also perform it at home. The Connection Between Taraweeh and the Qur’an
The Qur’an was revealed during Laylat-ul-Qadr in Ramadan, making Taraweeh a great way to honor the Qur’an.
Many mosques complete the entire Qur’an over 29-30 nights of Ramadan through Taraweeh recitations.
Hadith on Taraweeh 📖 Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) said:
"Whoever stands (in prayer) during the nights of Ramadan with faith and in the hope of reward, his previous sins will be forgiven."
(Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim)

Benefits of Praying Taraweeh ✔ Increases spirituality and devotion.
✔ Strengthens the connection with the Qur’an.
✔ Helps in seeking forgiveness from Allah.
✔ Encourages unity as Muslims gather to pray together.
✔ Provides physical benefits, as it involves extended periods of standing and bowing.
Conclusion Taraweeh is a blessed opportunity for Muslims to increase their worship, seek forgiveness, and strengthen their faith during Ramadan. Whether performed in congregation at the mosque or alone at home, it is a prayer that carries immense rewards and should not be missed!
