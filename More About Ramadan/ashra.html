The Three Ashras of Ramadan (رمضان کے تین عشرے)

Ramadan is divided into three Ashras (عشرہ), each lasting ten days. The word Ashra means "ten", and each Ashra carries its own spiritual significance, blessings, and supplications.
1️⃣ First Ashra – The Ashra of Mercy (رحمت کا عشرہ)
📅 Days: 1st – 10th of Ramadan 🔹 Theme: Seeking the Mercy of Allah (رحمت طلب کرنا)
🔹 Dua:
"رَبِّ اغْفِرْ وَارْحَمْ وَأَنْتَ خَيْرُ الرَّاحِمِينَ"
"O my Lord! Forgive and have mercy, for You are the Best of the merciful."
(Surah Al-Mu’minun 23:118)

Importance:
The first ten days of Ramadan are dedicated to seeking Allah’s mercy (رحمت).
It is a time to increase worship, recite the Qur’an, and ask for Allah’s kindness.
We should pray for ourselves, our families, and the entire Muslim Ummah.
It is an opportunity to develop a strong connection with Allah and seek His blessings.
💡 How to Make the Most of the First Ashra:
✅ Pray Tahajjud and additional Sunnah prayers.
✅ Recite Qur’an and make frequent Dhikr (remembrance of Allah).
✅ Show kindness and mercy to others.
✅ Help the needy through charity (Sadaqah & Zakat).

2️⃣ Second Ashra – The Ashra of Forgiveness (مغفرت کا عشرہ)
📅 Days: 11th – 20th of Ramadan
🔹 Theme: Seeking Forgiveness from Allah (مغفرت طلب کرنا)
🔹 Dua: "أَسْتَغْفِرُ اللَّهَ رَبِّي مِنْ كُلِّ ذَنْبٍ وَأَتُوبُ إِلَيْهِ"
"I seek forgiveness from Allah, my Lord, for all my sins, and I turn to Him in repentance." Importance:
The second ten days focus on asking Allah for forgiveness (مغفرت).
It is a chance to repent sincerely for past sins and make a fresh start.
Allah is Al-Ghaffar (The Most Forgiving), and He promises to forgive those who seek His mercy with sincerity. 📖 Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) said:
"Whoever sincerely asks for forgiveness, Allah will forgive him, even if his sins are as much as the foam of the sea."
(Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim)

💡 How to Make the Most of the Second Ashra:
✅ Repent sincerely (Tawbah) and seek forgiveness (Istighfar).
✅ Increase prayers, charity, and kindness.
✅ Avoid sins, backbiting, and arguments.
✅ Strengthen the bond with Allah through extra worship.

3️⃣ Third Ashra – The Ashra of Salvation from Hellfire (جہنم سے نجات کا عشرہ)
📅 Days: 21st – 30th of Ramadan
🔹 Theme: Seeking Protection from Hellfire (جہنم سے نجات)
🔹 Dua:
"اللَّهُمَّ أَجِرْنِي مِنَ النَّارِ"
"O Allah, save me from the Hellfire."
Importance:
This is the most blessed and powerful Ashra as it includes Laylat-ul-Qadr (شب قدر).
Laylat-ul-Qadr is better than a thousand months, and worship on this night is highly rewarded.
This Ashra is about seeking protection from the Hellfire and striving for entry into Jannah.
📖 Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) said:
"There is a night in Ramadan which is better than a thousand months. Whoever prays on this night with sincerity, all his past sins will be forgiven."
(Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim)

💡 How to Make the Most of the Third Ashra:
✅ Seek Laylat-ul-Qadr in the last ten nights.
✅ Pray Taraweeh and Tahajjud.
✅ Increase Dua and Dhikr.
✅ Give Sadaqah and Zakat generously.
✅ Recite the Qur’an and seek Allah’s closeness.

Conclusion;
The three Ashras of Ramadan provide a structured way to maximize the blessings of the holy month.
🔸 The First Ashra focuses on seeking Allah’s mercy.
🔸 The Second Ashra emphasizes asking for forgiveness.
🔸 The Third Ashra is dedicated to seeking salvation from Hellfire and finding Laylat-ul-Qadr.

By using these ten-day phases wisely, a Muslim can purify their soul, strengthen their faith, and attain Allah’s eternal blessings.

May Allah grant us mercy, forgiveness, and protection from Hellfire. 🤲💖✨
