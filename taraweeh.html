Taraweeh prayers are a significant part of the spiritual practices during the month of Ramadan. These are voluntary prayers that are performed after the Isha (night) prayer, specifically during the blessed month of Ramadan.

1. What is Taraweeh?
Taraweeh prayers are voluntary prayers, and they are performed in addition to the five daily obligatory prayers. It is a tradition of Prophet Muhammad (ï·º) to offer these prayers in the night during Ramadan. Taraweeh is an Arabic word that means "rest" because worshippers take short breaks after every four Rak'ahs.

2. Number of Rak'ahs
Taraweeh consists of 8 or 20 Rak'ahs (units of prayer), depending on the practice followed in different regions. In some places, 8 Rak'ahs are performed, while in others, 20 are prayed.

4. How to Perform Taraweeh
Taraweeh can be performed alone or in congregation, and the number of Rak'ahs can vary according to tradition. Generally, after performing Isha prayer, worshippers offer 2-4 Rak'ahs of Taraweeh at a time, taking short breaks between sets of four.
